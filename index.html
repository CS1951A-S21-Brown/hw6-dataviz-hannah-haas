Written Questions

1. Describe how your dashboard answers the questions presented. You don't have to address every question directly, but should at a high level address the main questions. (10 points)

My dashboard effectively answers all of the questions presented. My first graph is a bar graph. The x axis is the different genres and then the y axis is the number of titles (movies/tv shows). Therefore, it clearly shows the number of titles per genre on Netflix. My second graph is a line graph. The x axis is the year and the y axis is the average runtime of movies. This graph shows the average runtime of movies by release year over time. My third graph is a flow chart, specifically a network graph. Each node in the graph represents an actor. Specifically, it is a popular actor - one that has been in at least 6 movies. Each link between the nodes represents a movie that both connecting nodes have acted in. Additionally, this graph is broken down by rating. On default, it only shows the connections between actors in R rated movies. Additionally, the user has the choice of ratings R, PG-13, PG, G, and NR. I decided to limit it by ratings and also to popular actors to de-clutter the graph and to make the information more relevant. With showing all ratings together there are a lot of separate clusters because the actors don't cross ratings a lot - therefore by showing it broken down by ratings, you get more meaningful information. Therefore, this graph is easily able to show information about the casts and answer question #3.

2. List 3 reasons why D3 was helpful and improved your visualization (6 points)

D3 was definitely helpful and improved my visualization for a variety of reasons. Firstly, it is incredibly easy to use to make simple graphs. You simply have to compile the data and pass them in according to the D3 documentation. Secondly, there is a lot of built in functionality that you do not need to worry about. Typically, making a flow type graph would be incredibly difficult and time consuming. However, with D3, you simply provide the nodes and links and it produces the graph on the backend. Finally, it offers a lot of personalization for simple graphs. You can add colors, axis, lables, tooltips, and more. This enables users to make beautiful graphs!

3. List 3 reasons why D3 would not be the best tool for creating a visualization (6 points)

Despite the many benefits to D3 as I listed above, there are also some downsides to D3. Firstly, some of the documentation is uncompleted or confusing. Initially, I was planning on creating a Map graph for the Netflix dataset, however the documentation was confusing, assumed a lot of knowledge, and after a while I gave up. With this ineffective documentation, it's not always the best tool for creating visualizations. Secondly, I found D3 to be quite slow sometimes, particularily with my flow graph. While some of this may have been from my lack of efficiency, I researched and discovered that D3 is approx 70+ KB and therefore causes websites to load slower. Therefore, if speed is important, D3 might not be the best tool. Finally, it is a lot more complex and difficult to use for non-simple graphs. On the documentation there is pretty much no explanation on how to use D3 so I felt that it was very much look at the D3 documentation examples and the lab examples and try substitute your information to get it to work. Therefore, I sometimes felt that working with D3 is like trial and error. While this worked for the more trivial graphs, for more complex graphs I felt like the learning curve was very high.

Accessible Data Visualization

Evaluate the accessibility of your dashboard based on the readings in the “Before you begin” section. What kinds of users might find this dashboard accessible and who might have more difficulty? What additional actions might you take to make this dashboard more accessible to all audiences? Your response should refer to at least one of the readings and be about 1 paragraph.

Based on the readings, my evaluation of the accessibility of my dashboard is quite poor. From the Medium reading, a lot of their points made don't apply to my dashboard - I don't use sound, color to distinguish points, animations, metaphors or symbols. Therefore, my dashboard isn't inaccessible for these reasons. My dashboard is, however, not fully accessible to individuals with a visual impairment. While my dashboard does use SVG and there is a better experience for screen readers (as explained in the Betterment article), there are several actions I could take to make it less difficult. I could provide a summary for each of my graphs that is readable by screen reader, enabling individuals to get a quick understanding of the data. Secondly, I could provide the raw data that I use in my graphs. To do this, I could write to csv files when I calculate the data for my graphs and then link the files on my website. These actions would make my dashboard more accessible to blind individuals and individuals with visual impairments.

Reflect on the stages of your design and implementation process when you could have taken steps to make your dashboard more accessible to all audiences. What are some factors that kept you from taking these steps? (a few sentences)

When initially designing my dashboard I chose the graphs that made the most sense for the data and so that I could sufficiently complete the project objectives. Therefore, I chose a flow chart with a hoverable element. This is very inaccessible and therefore in the design process I could have taken steps to think of a better graph type to convey the same information. However, the factor of that it is required to have a hoverable element for the assignment kept me from being able to make this part accessible. Furthermore, in the implementation stage I could have taken the steps to include csv files with the graph's data and added graph summaries. Some of the factors that preventing me from taking these steps are laziness, time constraints, and lack of necessity. These are all terrible factors, but as a busy college student I felt that I didn't have the time to spend to make it as accessible as I could. However, in a non college setting, I think that I would try to prioritize accessibility and make time for it, and in that sense I don't think there are any valid factors that would prevent me from taking the steps.
